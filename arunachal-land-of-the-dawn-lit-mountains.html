Arunachal - Land of the dawn-lit mountains

18 Jul 2020 by Abrega Slona
Report

Arunachal Pradesh is India’s north-eastern most state and a tourism jewel in the rough. While the inner line permit and difficulty of access make visitation a somewhat tedious affair, once there, the domestic or international traveller is truly rewarded by the experience. The people are warm and welcoming, traditional and well-cultured, they are little changed by modern India and still continue with their age-old traditions. The weather is lovely year-round, the forests are green and salubrious, the wildlife diverse and engaging,the birdlife fabulous, and above all - the geography is diverse and unique. I have been visiting Arunachal Pradesh since 1998 and ever since I discovered the Macaca muzala in Arunachal, the first and only new species of simian to have been discovered worldwide in the recent past, I kept returning for more! I ended up creating a lot of High Definition media on the state with a number of documentary film offerings. This TVC is my offering to the state, in an attempt to bring rhe sophisticated world traveller to Arunachal, to see, smell, hear and discover the state’s secrets and recognize it as a valuable and integral part of the Indian union. After all, a good percentage of the global jet stream is contributed by the rich rain forests of Arunachal!
