Police swoop in to quickly arrest Extinction Rebellion protesters on second day of demonstrations

06 Sep 2020 by Abrega Slona
Report

Dozens of police officers aided in quickly arresting protesters from Extinction Rebellion on Wednesday (September 2), the second day of the group’s September "rebellion".
The protesters had planned to disrupt Prime Minister Boris Johnson’s entry to PMQs, but police prevented them from doing so. Instead, the PM was able to attend the weekly session and left via a side entrance.
The climate change group plan to continue protesting this week and into next week.
