A group of protesters in St. Louis walked on a private street on Sunday to demonstrate outside the mayor's home.  <br>The protesters opposed Mayor Lyda Krewson's decision to publish the names and addresses of people in favor of police reform.  <br>According to CNN, two armed individuals came out of a home near the mayor's house, brandishing weapons as the protesters walked by.  <br>Videos show Mark McCloskey armed with a long rifle, and spouse Patricia McCloskey with a handgun, in a tense standoff lasting roughly ten minutes.  <br>The couple says that while they supported the protesters, they were responding to some of them who threatened their lives.  <br>Police are investigating the incident. On Friday, the mayor apologized on Twitter, saying she didn't mean to hurt anyone.
