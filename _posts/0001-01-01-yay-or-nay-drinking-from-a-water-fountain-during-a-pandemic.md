So far, there is no evidence that COVID-19 can be transmitted by the water from water fountains.  <br>But according to HuffPost, experts say the virus may linger on surfaces.  <br>Therefore, it's best to avoid fountains if you can, or to limit any direct contact when using them.  <br>Filling a water bottle from a fountain, instead of drinking from it, means you won't leave saliva on the fountain. That makes it safer for you and for others.  <br>The CDC says schools and businesses should check fountains for other safety issues before they reopen.  <br>Prolonged closures could increase the risk for Legionnaires’ and other diseases, as standing water in plumbing systems can create bacteria growth.
