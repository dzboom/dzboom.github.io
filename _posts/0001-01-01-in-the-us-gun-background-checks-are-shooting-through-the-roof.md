New data from the Federal Bureau of Investigation reveals Americans have been extremely busy exercising their constitutional right to bear arms.  <br>According to CNN, the FBI conducted more than 3.6 million firearm background checks in July.  <br>By comparison, the bureau conducted just over 2 million checks in July 2019.  <br>That makes it the third-highest month on record for checks since the bureau began keeping statistics in 1998.  <br>Nevertheless, the order of states with the greatest number of applications has remained the same.  <br>Illinois, Kentucky, Texas, Florida, and California continually see the most background checks for the sale, transfer, or licensing of guns.  <br>The US has been dealing with a global pandemic and seeing some rioters causing damage after hijacking peaceful protests demanding racial justice.  <br>However, the order of states topping the list hasn't changed.
