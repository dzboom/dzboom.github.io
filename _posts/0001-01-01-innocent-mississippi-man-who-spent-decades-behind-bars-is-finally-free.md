Mississippi man Curtis Flowers was tried for the same 1996 quadruple murder six times.  <br>He spent nearly two dozen years behind bars and was sentenced to death four times. Now, Curtis Flowers is officially free.  <br>Newser reports Flowers was released on bail in December, after the US Supreme Court overturned his most recent conviction.  <br>Until January, the same prosecutor, Doug Evans, had attended to Flowers' case through all six trials.  <br>Evans recused himself from the case earlier this year. Flowers, meanwhile, has always maintained his innocence.  <br>I am finally free from the injustice that left me locked in a box for 23 years. Curtis Flowers
