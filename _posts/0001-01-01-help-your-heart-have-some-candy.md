There's one bit of bright news in a world struggling to defeat the novel coronavirus COVID-19 pandemic.  <br>According to CNN, a study from Houston's Baylor College of Medicine has shown that eating dark chocolate actually helps the heart.  <br>The study found eating chocolate more than once a week was associated with an 8% decreased risk of coronary artery disease.  <br>Containing less sugar and fat than milk or white chocolate, the healthiest choice is likely dark chocolate candy or bars, and Dutch-processed cocoa powder.  <br>Chocolate helps keep plaque from building up in blood vessels and reduces blood pressure. It's also been shown to help prevent stroke and heart failure.  <br>Chocolate also improves blood flow to the brain, which may help with cognitive function. It might even boost oxygen delivery during fitness training.  <br>Alas, it doesn't appear to be so good for the skin -- a recent study found a link to acne.
