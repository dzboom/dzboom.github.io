Florida hit a grim milestone on Saturday. According to HuffPost, the state's count of new novel coronavirus COVID-19 exceeded that of New York's.  <br>Nationwide, cases increased by 74,848, the second-highest daily count on record. Total COVID-19 deaths in the country topped 1,000 for the fourth consecutive day.  <br>New York chalked up an additional 750 cases for a total of 411,200 by the end of the day Friday, with a death toll of 33,600.  <br>At the same time, Florida added 12,199 cases, totaling 414,511 cases and 5,894 deaths, dying from COVID-19 at a rate of about one person every 10 minutes.  <br>Republican Gov. Ron DeSantis says Florida has stabilized its number of cases is 'definitely trending in a better direction.'  <br>Despite the surging number of cases, President Donald Trump and Gov. DeSantis continue to push for children to head back to school in the fall.  <br>More than 146,000 Americans have died of COVID-19, and more than 4.1 million have been infected.
