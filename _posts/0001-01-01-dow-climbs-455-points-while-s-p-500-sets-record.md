US stocks climbed on Wednesday with the S&amp;P 500 and Nasdaq composite extending records.  <br>According to Business Insider, both indexes closed at all-time highs on Tuesday.  <br>The rally was partly spurred by sectors that have underperformed in 2020, including utilities and financials.  <br>Traders are closely watching for signs that Congress will sign on for another pandemic stimulus bill soon.  <br>Treasury Secretary Steven Mnuchin on Tuesday encouraged Congress to pass stimulus measures.  <br>However, Democrats and Republicans remain locked in a stalemate over the next bill.
