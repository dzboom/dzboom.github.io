Local, state, and federal governments worldwide have generally tried to set an example in battling the novel coronavirus pandemic.  <br>So when the powerful or influential break the rules, it provokes fierce public anger and puts society's inequalities on full view.  <br>EU Commissioner Phil Hogan broke Ireland's coronavirus restrictions by attending a political golf society event with 80 other people.  <br>According to CNN, Hogan was just the latest member of the political elites to be caught flouting the rules.  <br>New Zealand's health minister, David Clark, was forced to resign after breaking the country's stay-at-home order to take his family to the beach.  <br>Social distancing also appears to have been largely disregarded at Republican National Convention events in the United States this past week.  <br>More than 1,000 people gathered on a White House lawn, few wearing masks and sitting close together.  <br>They were there to hear President Donald Trump accept his party's nomination Thursday night.  <br>When the rich and powerful flout the rules, it has only encouraged more division within the public over whether to observe public health guidelines.
