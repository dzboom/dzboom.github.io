90% or more of people undergoing total knee or hip replacement in 2017 received a prescription.  <br>The prescription was for opioid pain relievers within 60 days of surgery, reports UPI.  <br>This is up from 82% three years earlier, an 8% difference.  <br>This increase occurred despite efforts to discourage opioid use because of the risk of addiction.  <br>The dramatic increase persists in the face of myriad government and health system policies.  <br>Many guidelines are aimed at reducing opioid over-prescribing but it doesn't seem to curb it.
