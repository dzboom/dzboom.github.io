The virus most often behind the common cold is capable of preventing the flu virus.  <br>It prevents the flu from infecting airways by jump-starting the body's immune defenses.  <br>A study published Friday by The Lancet Microbe found this evidence recently.  <br>They did an analysis of more than 13,000 patients with symptoms of a respiratory infection.  <br>Those who had rhinovirus were not simultaneously infected with the flu virus.  <br>Now researchers want to determine if rhinovirus offers similar protective effects against COVID-19.
