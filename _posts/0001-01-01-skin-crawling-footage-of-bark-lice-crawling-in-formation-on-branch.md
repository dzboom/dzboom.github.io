Skin-crawling footage shows tiny bark lice travelling on a shrub together in Sullivan, Missouri, on July 2.  <br>An adult bark louse also crawls and follows the herd of nymphs travelling the bush.  <br>Insect enthusiast Eva Wiedeman said she found the little creatures while walking in the garden.  <br>She said: &quot;I always go outside to look for insects, then I saw these creatures together.&quot;  <br>Also called tree cattle, bark lice exhibit the fascinating behaviour of moving in groups, somewhat like a flock of sheep or herd of cattle.
