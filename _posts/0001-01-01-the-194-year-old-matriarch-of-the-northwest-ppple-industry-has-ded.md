An apple tree thought to be the oldest in the Pacific Northwest has died.  <br>this summer at 194 years of age. The Old Apple Tree in Vancouver, Washington was 194 years old.  <br>The tree is believed to have been planted in 1826 when fur traders of the Hudson's Bay Company settled in the area.  <br>The tree is considered the matriarch of the area's thriving apple industry.  <br>According to CNN, the tree produced a green apple that was bitter to the taste but great for baking.  <br>&quot;While we knew this day would come, we hoped it was still years away&quot;.  <br>Charles Ray  <br>Urban forester for the City of Vancouver
