Immunity against COVID-19 in people who have had and recovered from the disease may fade after 90 days.  <br>The data is from a small study that looked at 34 patient's antibodies following infection with and recovery from COVID-19.  <br>According to UPI, the analysis evaluated patients ranging in age from 21 to 68 years old.  <br>Researchers found that subjects produced antibodies from as short as 44 days to as long as 119 days.  <br>Researchers said that their results suggest immunity may be &quot;relatively short-lived and immunity may wane.&quot;  <br>researchers also said that the findings need to be confirmed in a larger study, with more patients.
