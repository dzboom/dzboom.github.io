A magnitude 6.8 earthquake shakes the home of a terrified family in Copiapo, northern Chile, on Monday morning (September 1).  <br>The quake struck near the coast of northern Chile shortly after midnight at 12:09 a.m. local time.  <br>Chile’s seismological agency reported that the initial earthquake measured 7.0 and struck around 31 kilometers below the seafloor.  <br>It is not known if there are any casualties. The Pacific Tsunami Warning Center said there was no tsunami threat from the earthquake.
