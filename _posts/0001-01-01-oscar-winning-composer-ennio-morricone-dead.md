Ennio Morricone, the Oscar-winning film composer, died at the age of 91, his lawyer told CNN Monday.  <br>He died at dawn in a Rome hospital after falling and breaking his leg, his lawyer, Giorgio Assumma, said.  <br>Morricone is best known internationally as the composer behind the instantly recognizable melodies from &quot;The Good, the Bad and the Ugly.&quot;  <br>The Italian composer was famous for the tension-filled scores of spaghetti Westerns.  <br>He won an Academy Award for his soundtrack for Quentin Tarantino's &quot;The Hateful Eight&quot; in 2016, after five previous nominations.
