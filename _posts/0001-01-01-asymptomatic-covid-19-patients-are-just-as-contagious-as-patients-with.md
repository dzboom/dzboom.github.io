People with confirmed COVID-19 who are not showing any symptoms of the disease are as contagious as those with symptoms.  <br>According to.a new study, asymptomatic COVID-19 patients can test positive for the virus for up to 18 days after diagnosis.  <br>In samples collected from patients, the virus declined more slowly in those without symptoms than in those with symptoms.  <br>Many asymptomatic patients also had evidence of &quot;viral shedding&quot;, meaning they were contagious for at least 30 days after diagnosis.
