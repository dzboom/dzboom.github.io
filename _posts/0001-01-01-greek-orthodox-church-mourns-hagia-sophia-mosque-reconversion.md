Churches around Greece were in &quot;mourning&quot; on Friday (July 24) as Muslim prayers were to be held at Hagia Sophia in Istanbul for the first time in decades.  <br>Church bells around the country pealed at midday with their flags at half-mast to protest what the head of the Church of Greece, Archbishop Ieronymos, has called an &quot;unholy act of defiling&quot; of the former Byzantine Empire cathedral.  <br>&quot;(Today) is a day of mourning for all of... Christianity,&quot; Ieronymos said.  <br>The archbishop said he would hold a special service at the Athens Metropolis in the evening and chant the Akathist Hymn in honour of the Virgin Mary.  <br>The same service was held in Hagia Sophia on the eve of the Byzantine's Empire's fall to the Ottomans in 1453.  <br>Hagia Sophia is &quot;a symbol of our faith and a universal monument of culture,&quot; Ieronymos said  <br>Religious and nationalist groups are expected to hold protests in Athens and Thessaloniki later on Friday.
