Arizona Gov. Doug Ducey said Monday that bars, gyms, movie theaters, waterparks, and tubing rentals will shut down for 30 days.  <br>The move is designed to prevent overloading Arizona's health system, which is nearing capacity in its use of intensive care unit beds.  <br>According to Business Insider, coronavirus cases are continuing to skyrocket in the southwestern state. Arizona currently has over 74,000 coronavirus cases.  <br>The state will also stop issuing special event licenses from June 29 to July 27. Events with more than 50 people are banned starting on June 29.  <br>We must be clear-eyed. The next few weeks will be hard. But these steps combined with stepped-up compliance with public health guidance can make a difference, and we're grateful to Arizonans for their cooperation. Stay home. Wear a mask. Be responsible. Arizona Gov. Doug Ducey
