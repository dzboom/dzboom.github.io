The common cold might help produce immunity against COVID-19, reports UPI.  <br>This may even be true in people who have not been infected with the new coronavirus.  <br>A study published Tuesday by the journal Science details the findings.  <br>This immune response is likely a function of a phenomenon called &quot;T cell memory.&quot;  <br>T cell memory could help explain why some people don't get as sick as others from COVID-19.  <br>Those with T cells that fight the common cold have success because the coronavirus is a type of cold.
