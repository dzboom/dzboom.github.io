Two escaped ostriches shocked locals when they rampaged through a street in the Philippines this afternoon (Aug 4).  <br>The huge birds were being kept as pets in a back garden in an upmarket part of Quezon City area of Manila when they broke free from their enclosures.   <br>Confused residents and passing drives watched as they made bids for freedom - galloping down the concrete streets.    <br>Shocked onlooker Dan Rivera saw one of the birds on his way home running along an intersection before a stray dog started barking at it.  <br>He said: &quot;I feel like I entered the movie Jumanji where animals run freely through the city. It was a weird experience.&quot;  <br>The other ostrich was found roaming near the guard house of the gated community.   <br>Mark Umipig, another resident, said the bird could not leave the estate because of a toll gate barrier that blocks the pathway.  <br>He said: &quot;The guard jokingly refused for the bird to leave because it did not have a quarantine pass.&quot;  <br>The two birds reportedly escaped their cage when it was accidentally left open by their owner who lives in the private road.  <br>Several maintenance and security crew helped to catch the swift birds and gave dragged them  back to the owner.  <br>Ostriches - the largest and heaviest of all living birds - are only found in the wild in Africa.   <br>However, the flightless creatures can be kept in captivity as pets in some parts of the world.
