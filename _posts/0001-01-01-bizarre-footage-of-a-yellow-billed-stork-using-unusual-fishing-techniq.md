This was the bizarre moment a yellow-billed stork used its beak and feet to catch fish in Kruger National Park.  <br>The clip filmed on January 29, shows the stork bending down, balancing on one leg with its mouth open catching prey in its mouth.  <br>Nature enthusiast Bob Humphries told Newsflare: &quot;They wade with the bill partly immersed and held slightly agape, often stirring the sediment with one foot in an attempt to flush prey.  As this adult demonstrates, they often shade the water with an outstretched wing on the same side as foot used for stirring the bottom mud.&quot;
