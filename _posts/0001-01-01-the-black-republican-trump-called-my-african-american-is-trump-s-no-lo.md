In June, 2016, Gregory Cheadle attended a rally by then-presidential candidate Donald Trump in Redding, California.  <br>Cheadle stood out as a Black Republican, and even more so since Trump referred to him as 'my African-American.'  <br>Now 63, Cheadle is a real estate broker and a volunteer at a hospital emergency room.  <br>According to CNN, Cheadle now says he is a very different man than the one who went to hear Trump four years ago.  <br>Cheadle says he has since lost respect for some Black Republican conservatives, comparing them to ventriloquists' dolls: Black puppets controlled by Whites.  <br>He also says he was deflated by how the Republican party reacted to the death of former GOP presidential candidate Herman Cain.  <br>Cain, who was Black, died after contracting coronavirus soon after attending a Trump rally without wearing a mask.  <br>It was sad that he died, but even more sad that he was not given any honor by the Republican Party. It was like, 'He's dead. No problem. Goodbye.' Gregory Cheadle
