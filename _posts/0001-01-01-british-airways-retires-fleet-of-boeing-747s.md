According to CNN Business, British Airways will retire 31 Boeing 747 jets, four years ahead of schedule.  <br>The coronavirus pandemic has wiped out the demand for air travel, and many airlines are cutting the size of their fleets and scrapping older aircraft.  <br>British Airways is the world's biggest operator of the 747-400.   <br>In a statement on Friday, they said, &quot;It is with great sadness that we can confirm we are proposing to retire our entire 747 fleet with immediate effect.&quot;  <br>The once groundbreaking jumbo jet was one of the most popular planes in the world.   <br>British Airways follows other airlines like Lufthansa, who is cutting 100 aircraft from its fleet.   <br>They grounded all 14 of its A380s in March, with no plans to get them back in the air anytime soon.
