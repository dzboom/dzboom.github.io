CNN reports a significant proportion of adults all over the world say they have major concerns about receiving a vaccination for the novel coronavirus.  <br>Neil Johnson is a physicist at George Washington University who is studying vaccine skepticism on social media.  <br>Johnson says among people who aren't anti-vaxxers, but are dubious about a COVID-19 vaccine, have four commonly held objections.  <br>The first is whether the vaccine is safe; the second is whether the vaccine is actually necessary.  <br>The third concern surrounds trust in the establishment and the pharmaceutical companies producing the vaccines.  <br>The fourth major concern is the perceived uncertainty in the science of immunology.
