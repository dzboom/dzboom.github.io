A new study has revealed disturbing information about how the novel coronavirus COVID-19 can affect the heart.  <br>According to UPI, 78% of 100 people diagnosed with COVID-19 showed evidence of heart damage caused by the disease, weeks after they recovered.  <br>Prior to the study, none of the patients had experienced heart problems before contracting COVID-19 and were mostly healthy.  <br>However, after recovering from the virus, 78 of them showed evidence of heart damage, revealed by magnetic resonance imaging.  <br>The researchers said the MRI findings were consistent with two potentially serious heart conditions: myocarditis and pericarditis.  <br>Myocarditis is inflammation of the heart muscle. As it can reduce the heart's ability to pump, it can potentially cause irregular heartbeats.  <br>Pericarditis inflames the protective tissues surrounding the heart and can cause pain.
