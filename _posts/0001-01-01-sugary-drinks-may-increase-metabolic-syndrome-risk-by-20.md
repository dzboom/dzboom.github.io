Sugary drinks can increase a person's risk for metabolic syndrome by up to 20%.  <br>A review of data from 13 previous studies warns against consuming large amounts of fructose, or fruit sugar.  <br>Metabolic syndrome is a group of conditions including high blood pressure, elevated blood sugar, and excess body fat.  <br>According to UPI, the syndrome also includes increased cholesterol and triglyceride levels.  <br>Collectively, these conditions increase your risk for Type 2 diabetes and heart disease.  <br>Not all sources of fructose are harmful. Consumption of yogurt and fresh fruit doesn't carry the same risk.
