The Bangkok Motor Show opened Wednesday (July 15) - the first car festival to be staged since the start of the coronavirus pandemic.  <br>There had been concerns about whether the festival, which sees stands from the biggest car manufacturers in the world, could go ahead due to the virus.  <br>From Detroit to Geneva, motor shows have been forced to cancel due to the Covid-19 pandemic, throwing the future of the industry traditional way of marketing new models into doubt.  <br>However, with no new local transmission of Covid-19 within Thailand for more than 50 days, organisers were given the green light.  <br>The 41st Bangkok International Motor Show opened to the public after being pushed back twice since March.  <br>Event organiser Prachin Eamlumnow said: 'This is more than the motor show, but also Thailand reputation because the other event organisers will be watching.'  <br>Thailand will be the first to host a motor show on this scale since the pandemic, he said.  <br>Thailand is a major regional car production hub, with its previous motor shows registering more than a million visitors.  <br>Organisers have pledged to limit crowds this year and control entry at the show, where 25 car brands - including Ford and Subaru - and 22 motorcycle manufacturers will display their products.
