The great state of Michigan has been notorious for its eye-wateringly high car insurance rates.  <br>But that's about to change in July, when a new law will drop the requirement for expensive unlimited personal injury protection (PIP) coverage.  <br>Shopping around for car insurance or dropping the coverage could help Michigan drivers save at least $120 per year, per car.  <br>But according to Business Insider, you don't have to live in Michigan to get a break on your car insurance rate.  <br>Not only can states change their laws, but car insurance companies are constantly changing their algorithms.  <br>Your age, driving history, type of car, and even your gender can influence the price you'll pay for car insurance.  <br>For new drivers with their first-ever policy, shopping around after six months of coverage could also help lower your rate.  <br>Take an hour to shop around. It can help you stay on top of those changes, and continue to get the best rate for you.  <br>Look for the most coverage for the smallest premium, and you'll find the best deal. Good luck!
