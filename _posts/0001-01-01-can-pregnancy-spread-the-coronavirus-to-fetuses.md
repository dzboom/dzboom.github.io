Can a pregnant woman spread the coronavirus to her fetus?  <br>It’s possible, but it seems to be relatively rare and scientists think they know why that is.  <br>Many viruses can cross the placenta and infect a fetus in the womb, reports Huffpost.  <br>Researchers in Italy studied 31 women with COVID-19 who delivered babies in March and April.  <br>They found COVID in several samples of umbilical cord blood, the placenta and, in one case, breast milk.  <br>But this sort of testing can just detect bits of genetic material, not if the virus is causing infection.
