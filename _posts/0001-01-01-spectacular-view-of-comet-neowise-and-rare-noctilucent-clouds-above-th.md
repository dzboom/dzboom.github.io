Stargazers in the UK have been looking to the skies recently, in order to secure their glimpse of Comet Neowise, that is showing over UK skies.   <br>This time lapse footage was captured at a rural beauty spot near Harrogate this morning(11th July)  <br>Comet Neowise was visible with the naked eye, from Almscliff Crag and can easily be seen above the horizon in the northern sky from midnight until an hour before sunrise.  <br>The comet is due to be around until the end of this month and will get higher above the horizon each night.  <br>Viewers looking at Neowise this morning were also treated to spectacular display from Noctilucent clouds.  <br>A statement by NASA...  <br>Nasa said: &quot;A comet has suddenly become visible to the unaided eye.  <br>&quot;Comet C/2020 F3 (NEOWISE) was discovered in late March and brightened as it reached its closest approach to the Sun, inside the orbit of Mercury, late last week.  <br>&quot;The interplanetary iceberg survived solar heating, so far, and is now becoming closer to the Earth as it starts its long trek back to the outer Solar System.  <br>&quot;As Comet NEOWISE became one of the few naked-eye comets of the 21st Century, word spread quickly, and the comet has already been photographed behind many famous sites and cities around the globe.&quot;
