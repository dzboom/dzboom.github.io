E-cigarette maker Juul is starting to choke.  <br>According to Gizmodo, Juul is preparing for more layoffs, and may ultimately pull out of some overseas markets.  <br>The company is in the midst of dealing with regulatory crackdowns and multiple investigations into its products and marketing tactics.  <br>Juul flew close to the sun a few years ago when it dominated the market for vape products, but has seen a spectacular fall from its prime.  <br>Juul's incredibly popular fruity flavors were pulled from sale as health officials said the pods contributed to an epidemic of youth nicotine use.  <br>In response, Juul has considered a number of methods to try to curb the sale to and use of its products by minors, including with age-locked verification tools.
