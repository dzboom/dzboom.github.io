Republican Sen. Tom Cotton of Arkansas has described slavery as a &quot;necessary evil&quot;.  <br>The 1619 Project is an initiative from The New York Times, according to reports CNN.  <br>It reframes American history of August 1619, when the first slave ship arrived on America's shores.  <br>&quot;As the founding fathers said, it was the necessary evil upon which the union was built.&quot; Tom Cotton  <br>Cotton then called the 1619 Project &quot;a racially divisive, revisionist account of history.&quot;  <br>Cotton now claims he was citing the views of America's founding fathers, rather than his own.  <br>However, Cotton introduced legislation to prevent funding for schools that teach the 1619 Project.  <br>&quot;(The 1619 Project) denies the noble principles of freedom and equality on which our nation was founded. Not a single cent of federal funding should go to indoctrinate young Americans with this left-wing garbage.&quot;  <br>There is no record of any Founding Fathers arguing US slavery was a &quot;necessary evil.&quot;
