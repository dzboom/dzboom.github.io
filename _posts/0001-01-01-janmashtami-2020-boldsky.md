Preparations for the birth anniversary of Shri Krishna have intensified. However, this time due to Corona virus epidemic, there will be no big events in the temples. Janmashtami is being celebrated on both August 11 and August 12. According to Hinduism beliefs, Shri Krishna was born on the eighth day of the Krishna Paksha of Bhadrapada. On the day of Janmashtami people worship fast with Bhajan-Kirtan and Vidhi-Vidhan to keep the fast to get the blessings of Lord Krishna. According to astrologers there was Ashtami Tithi and Rohini Nakshatra at 12 o'clock at the time of the birth of Lord Shri Krishna.
