Closing schools across the US in March reduced the number of COVID-19 cases and deaths.  <br>Doctors and scientists have reported the reduction was by as much as 60% in some areas.  <br>Some states saw a lower number of confirmed infections at the time of school closure.  <br>They also likely saw the biggest positive impact of children remaining at home.  <br>These states cut cases by an estimated 72%, according to reports at UPI.  <br>These states may have reduced their total number of infections by 49% by shutting schools.
