Sheard released her sixth album Kierra. Debuting at number one on the Billboard Top Gospel Albums chart with 6,000 equivalent album units, it became her fifth chart-topping album, replacing Kanye West's Jesus is King which had held the number one spot for 6 months since November 9, 2019. Sheard co-wrote the entire album, which includes a mix of studio and live tracks recorded in her hometown, Detroit.[31] The album spawned 3 top 20 singles on the Hot Gospel Songs chart; &quot;Don't Judge Me&quot;, &quot;It Keeps Happening&quot; and &quot;Something Has To Break&quot; (featuring Tasha Cobbs Leonard), the latter peaking at number 5.
