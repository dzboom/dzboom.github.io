Authorities in Tokyo believe many of the city's infections are happening when people go out at night.  <br>They have requested restaurants and bars that serve alcohol to close at 10 p.m.  <br>The government has also made a substantial financial commitment to battling the virus' impact.  <br>They have pumped more than $2 trillion into the economy to help stave off a collapse.  <br>Prime Minister Shinzo Abe said Thursday he would not call for a state of emergency.  <br>More infections are being identified now than during the first state of emergency in April.
