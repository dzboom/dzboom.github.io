Domestic veteran Rajat Bhatia announced retirement from all forms of cricket and he looks back at his long career with &quot;no regrets&quot; after coming close to play for India in 2014. The 40-year-old all-rounder started his career with Tamil Nadu in 2003-04 but played most of his cricket for Delhi. In the 2018-19, he guided new entrants Uttarakhand to the Ranji Trophy quarterfinals.Bhatia, who was considered Delhi cricket's crisis man, played 112 first-class games, scoring 6482 runs at an average of 49.10 apart from taking 137 wickets at 27.97.
