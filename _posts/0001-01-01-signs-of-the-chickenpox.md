Getting the chickenpox is a right of passage for all children.  <br>The signs of chickenpox include fever, fatigue, headache, loss of appetite, and an itchy rash.  <br>The rash usually develops about 10 to 21 days after you are exposed to the virus.  <br>The other physical symptoms can occur a few days before the rash develops.   <br>Business Insider reports that the infection usually lasts 5 to 10 days.  <br>The virus goes away on its own, though certain high-risk groups will want to seek medication.
