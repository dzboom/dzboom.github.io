Joe Biden is running for President.  <br>He was doing well in all the polling, state wide and nationally.  <br>But, Biden has a history of gaffes.  <br>He's also not as sharp as he once was.  <br>At the end of August, Biden began seeing his double digit lead over President Donald Trump fade away.  <br>His lead is now half what it once was.  <br>The American Spectator thinks that Biden's campaign is ripe for a complete implosion.  <br>Overall, according to RealClearPolitics, Biden’s average lead in the all important battleground states has shrunken from 6.3 percent on July 28 to 3.3 percent.  <br>Biden's biggest appeal is to white collar under-educated voters, who progressives believe make all of Trump's base.  <br>But, they only make up 25% of Trump's voter base.  <br>Biden has made some critical mistakes in the last few weeks.  <br>He blamed President Trump for job losses caused by COVID-19.  <br>He has endorsed another nationwide lockdown, called for a universal mask mandate, and taken multiple positions on fracking.  <br>He also waited too long to condemn the violent protests gripping the country.  <br>The American Spectator believes these mistakes mark the inevitable implosion of a campaign built on a myth about Trump’s base.
