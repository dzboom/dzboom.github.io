Business Insider reports that Facebook will prohibit new political ads from running in the week leading up to Election Day.  <br>Facebook announced a two-pronged approach ahead of the upcoming presidential election.  <br>First, the social network said it will not allow new political ads to run in the week before the election in November.  <br>The Wall Street Journal said Facebook will flag posts from candidates claiming premature victory.  <br>Facebook will redirect users to vote tallies showing up-to-date results.  <br>This news comes after reports about tech companies meeting with government officials to discuss fighting election meddling.
