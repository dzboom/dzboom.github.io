This is the amazing moment a thirsty king cobra was given a drink of water after slithering onto a building site in Thailand.  <br>The parched serpent was seen sluggishly moving through the property in Chonburi, eastern Thailand, on July 10.  <br>Builder Jaraput Srichantuk noticed the dehydrated snake  and rather than flee - or attack it like some locals - he kindly tended to the deadly animal.  <br>Footage shows Jaraput calmly holding his water bottle to the king cobra's mouth as it gratefully laps up the liquid.   <br>Jaraput said the snake was around 14ft long but was tame because it appeared to have recently shed its skin.  <br>He said: &quot;I saw the snake was having difficulty in a neighbour's property so I rescued him and gave him some water.   <br>''I found his skin nearby and kept it as a souvenir to help me study snakes.  <br>''People should not interact closely with wild snakes like I did, but I am used to them and have doen it before.''
