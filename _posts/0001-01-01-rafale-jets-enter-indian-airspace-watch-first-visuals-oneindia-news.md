The Rafales entered Indian airspace, and here are the first visuals. The first batch of Rafale fighter jets that took off from France on Monday. The first batch of five Rafale fighter jets has landed at the Ambala airbase after covering a distance of nearly 7,000 km to join the Indian Air Force fleet. The fleet, comprising three single seater and two twin seater aircraft, will be part of the No. 17 Squadron of the Air Force, also known as the 'Golden Arrows'.
