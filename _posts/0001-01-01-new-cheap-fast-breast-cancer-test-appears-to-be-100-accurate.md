Researchers have developed a rapid, affordable, accurate test for breast cancer.  <br>A small study assessing the test's accuracy found that it provided the correct result 100% of the time.  <br>Additionally, the results are often in less than an hour, according to reports at UPI.  <br>The test, called CytoPAN, was designed for use in developing regions.  <br>Patients there often face long wait times for a diagnosis which negatively impacts their prognosis.  <br>However, if larger studies find that it's accurate, the new test could be used worldwide.  <br>The test would be a welcome alternative to the more expensive and invasive tests available now.
