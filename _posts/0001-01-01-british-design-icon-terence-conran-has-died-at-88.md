Famed British designer Terence Conran died peacefully at home on Saturday. He was 88.  <br>After studying textile design in London, Conran founded Habitat in 1964, which went on to become a national and international chain.  <br>Eight years later, the designer opened the first Conran Shop, then opened additional stores in cities including Paris and New York.  <br>Conran founded the Design Museum in London in 1989 and was also a famed restauranteur.  <br>According to CNN, the Design Museum paid tribute to Conran, saying he 'had a greater impact than any other designer of his generation.'  <br>Terence Conran has filled our lives for generations with ideas, innovation, and brilliant design. He is one of the most iconic figures of post-war Britain. Peter Mandelson Chairman of the Board of Trustees, The Design Museum
