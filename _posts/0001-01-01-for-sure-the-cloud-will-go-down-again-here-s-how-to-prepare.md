Most of the apps Americans rely on for work and play are run from the cloud, and are essential for getting pretty much anything done these days.  <br>But what happens when the cloud goes down? After all, Gizmodo reports cloud outages are frequent, even if they rarely all go down at once.  <br>First, secure your emails. You can enable access to your Gmail offline, or set them up to sync with a build-in desktop client, or one like Thunderbird.  <br>Sync your files with a different cloud, such as Dropbox, and configure them so you can access them offline, if your ISP is down.  <br>Finally, just give social media a rest. You won't miss it!
