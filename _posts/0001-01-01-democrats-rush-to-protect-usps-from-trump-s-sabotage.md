Two Senate Democrats late Thursday announced plans to introduce a bill, reports Gizmodo.  <br>They say it would halt any “abrupt operational changes” at the U.S. Postal Service.  <br>Concerns are rising that the Trump administration is attempting to interfere with the flow of mail-in ballots.  <br>The Delivering for America Act is co-sponsored by Sen. Gary Peters (D-MI) and Sen. Tom Carper (D-DE).  <br>Peters is a ranking member of the Homeland Security and Governmental Affairs Committee.  <br>The bill reportedly prohibits any changes to Postal Service standards that will impact the service.  <br>It also protects the speed of deliveries, closures or consolidation, and any reduction in facility hours.
