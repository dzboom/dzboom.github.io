Pop Smoke’s posthumous debut album, Shoot For the Stars, Aim For the Moon, made a huge splash on the Billboard 200 chart this week. Billboard reports that the late rapper’s album moved 251,000 equivalent album units in its first week, easily landing at No. 1. It’s the sixth-biggest week of 2020, and the first posthumous album to go No. 1 since XXXTENTACION’s Skins.
