Brazilian pro surfer Maya Gabeira was flattened by a colossal wave in Portugal which, according to the filmer, could possibly be a new world record.  <br>Footage filmed on February 11 in Nazaré, Portugal shows Gabeira ride the monster wave before being engulfed as it crashes down.  <br>This wave is currently one of three nominees for the Women's Biggest Wave that the World Surf League are considering. The award will be announced on August 17.  <br>Filmer Pedro Miranda told Newsflare: &quot;I’ve filmed both Maya’s World Record back in 2018 and to be honest, this year’s wave looked bigger at first sight.   <br>&quot;Maya charged the wave behind the peak and drawn a beautiful yet very aggressive line, she rode the most critical part of the wave on a flawless way, for sure one of the biggest and most spectacular waves I’ve ever captured out there.&quot;  <br>In 2018, Gabeira rode a 68-foot wave which was entered as the Guinness World Record for the largest wave surfed by a female.  <br>&quot;I don’t like to go into speculations, the official measurements will be presented by the WSL in just a few days, whoever wins, Maya or Justine, it will be fair, those girls are breaking the boundaries of the sport and it’s a pleasure to capture those special moments,&quot; Miranda added.
