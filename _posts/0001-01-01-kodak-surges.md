Shares of Kodak surged as much as 1,900% in two days.  <br>This is because Kodak was awarded a $765 million government loan to make drug ingredients in response to the pandemic.  <br>Business Insider reports Kodak plans to launch a pharmaceuticals division that will make essential drug components currently in &quot;chronic national shortage.&quot;  <br>The deal is being touted as &quot;one of the most important deals in the history of US pharmaceutical industries.&quot;  <br>Kodak's stock price tripled on Tuesday, then rose as much as 570% on Wednesday.
