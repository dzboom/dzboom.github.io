Hong Kong’s Secretary for Commerce and Economic Development Edward Yau on August 13, 2020, rejected US plans to order goods imported from the city to be labelled &quot;Made in China&quot;. Washington’s marking requirement would start from September, according to plans published on the US Federal Register by the Customs and Border Protection agency.   Video source: Facebook@news.gov.hk
