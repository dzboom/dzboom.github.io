A 1794 Silver Dollar believed to possibly the first to be struck by the US Mint is hitting the auction block.  <br>The coin known as the &quot;Flowing Hair&quot; dollar, features a portrait of Lady Liberty on one side and an eagle on the other.  <br>According to CNN, the last time it was up for auction, in 2013, it sold for $10 million.  <br>The current owner of the coin, Bruce Morelan is putting an assortment of rare coins up for sale.  <br>Morelan estimates the assortment of historic coins he's offering is valued at about $20 million.  <br>The sale of the Flowing Hair coin will be overseen by Legend Auctions on October 8th at The Venetian Hotel in Las Vegas.
