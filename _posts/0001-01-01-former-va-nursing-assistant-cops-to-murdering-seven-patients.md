A former nursing assistant in West Virginia admitted in court on Tuesday to intentionally killing seven patients.  <br>Reta Mays was a nursing assistant at the Louis A. Johnson VA Medical Center in Clarksburg.  <br>According to HuffPost, Mays was charged with seven counts of second-degree murder, for administering fatal doses of insulin to her victims.  <br>She was also charged with one count of assault with the intent to commit the murder of an eighth person. Mays faces life sentences for each murder.  <br>Between 2017 and 2018, the 46-year-old gave her victims unprescribed doses of insulin while she worked overnight shifts.
