Nurses try to cheer themselves up after a long shift at the hospital by playing a &quot;charge nurse elimination&quot; game.  <br>Nurse Rodney Lim said that the COVID-19 cases have spiked in their hometown Cebu City, Philippines so it was a relief for them to be assigned as the &quot;charge nurse.&quot;  <br>A charge nurse oversees the needs of the non-coronavirus patients in the hospital, as well as miscellaneous tasks such as checking the other staff and talking to a patient's family who has concerns.  <br>The task is physically tolling but most nurses prefer the role instead of being assigned to the COVID-19 ward.  <br>The team draw names from a hat and last person standing takes care of the charge nurse role.  <br>Rodney said: &quot;Being a charge nurse is tiring, but it feels like a rest day for us during this pandemic.&quot;  <br>In the video taken on July 25, Rodney and colleagues Hanna, Raquel, and Jacoba play the game by drawing lots.  <br>Rodney added: &quot;He said, &quot;We just want to have fun even for a while. We are not escaping our responsibilities since we love what we are doing as a nurse.  <br>&quot;We just do this to relieve our worries this pandemic is giving to us. As nurses, we live because we care.  <br>The number of COVID-19 cases in the Philippines has jumped to 115,980, with a death toll of 2,123
