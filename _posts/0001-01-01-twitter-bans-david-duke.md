CNN Business reports that Twitter permanently banned David Duke's account on Thursday night.   <br>The former Ku Klux Klan leader's ban comes after multiple violations of the company's hateful conduct policy.   <br>Twitter forbids  accounts that &quot;promote violence against or directly attack or threaten other people&quot; on the basis of personal characteristics.  <br>Earlier this week, Twitter said it would block and or suspend accounts and links sharing blocked or hateful content.   <br>It's unclear what specific action triggered Duke's suspension, but Twitter told CNN their decision was &quot;in line with our recently-updated guidance on harmful links.&quot;  <br>This week, Twitter also temporarily restricted Donald Trump Jr's account, after he posted a video which made false claims about coronavirus.
