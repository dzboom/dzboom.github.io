Rashida Tlaib is on the verge of losing her seat in the House of Representatives.  <br>Tlaib represents Michigan’s 13th Congressional District. She won it in 2018 after Rep. John Conyers retired.  <br>In 2018 Tlaib defeated Detroit City Council President Brenda Jones by 1 point.  <br>Now the two are facing off in the August 4th primary.  <br>Slate reports that Jones is set to annihilate Tlaib tat the ballot box.  <br>Last cycle, Tlaib and Jones were on the ballot with four other candidates.  <br>This time, those four other candidates are supporting Jones.  <br>Jones has been critical of Tlaib’s habit of putting her foot in her mouth.  <br>“There are things that I might feel, but I just don’t say in public and an example is ‘impeach the M-F’ on the very first day.  <br>There’s also a racial element to the race.  <br>The district is majority-black and local leaders feel it's time for a black woman to represent the district.  <br>Jones is a legend in Michigan politics.  <br>Even Tlaib's most ardent supporters admit that her paltry accomplishments pale in comparison to Jones.
