A team of Virginia Tech scientists has cracked the mystery of how snakes 'fly.'  <br>According to CNN, study lead author Isaac Yeaton said the team started out with a basic understanding of undulation.  <br>They discovered undulation stabilizes the glide, stops the snake from tumbling, and allows them to cover more horizontal distance.  <br>The researchers carried out indoor experiments with live snakes at Virginia Tech and also developed a computational model.  <br>The next stage for researchers is to observe snakes jumping from trees -- or gliding -- outdoors.  <br>We have a visceral response to snakes. Then the idea that this animal can then fly is very unsettling to people. Isaac Yeaton, Team Leader Virginia Tech
