Chetan Chauhan, Former India Cricketer And UP Minister, Passes Away Due To COVID-19 Complications

21 Aug 2020 by Abrega Slona
Report

Former Indian cricketer and UP minister Chetan Chauhan passed away after suffering multiple-organ failure on Sunday (August 16, 2020). Chauhan was put into ventilator support at the Medanta Hospital in Gurugram and after he suffered kidney failure on Friday. In July, the former opener had tested positive for COVID-19 virus and was admitted to the Sanjay Gandhi PGI hospital in Lucknow. He was yet to recover from the widely-spreading disease when he suffered kidney failure and blood pressure problems on Friday.
